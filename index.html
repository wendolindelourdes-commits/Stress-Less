Full Conversation Script

Emily: Hi, Jake! You look stressed. What's wrong?

Jake: Hi, Emily. Yes, I am. I have my final English exam next week, and I'm really worried. I have so much to study, and I don't know where to start.

Emily: Don't worry, Jake. First, you should make a study schedule. Planning your time helps you stay organized.

Jake: That's a good idea, but I always get distracted. I keep checking my phone every five minutes.

Emily: Ah, yes. The phone is a big distraction. You ought to limit your social media time. Maybe put your phone in another room while you study.

Jake: I'll try that. But what about when I feel tired? I sometimes study for four hours without stopping.

Emily: Four hours? That's too much, Jake! You must take short breaks. For example, study for fifty minutes and then rest for ten minutes. It keeps your mind fresh.

Jake: That makes sense. What about sleep? I usually stay up until 2:00 AM studying.

Emily: No, Jake! You shouldn't stay up late before the exam. You must get at least eight hours of sleep. A tired brain can't remember anything!

Jake: You are right. Thank you, Emily. Your advice is very helpful.

Emily: Anytime, Jake. Good luck! You'll do great.
